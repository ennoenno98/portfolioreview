# Vanatari Portfolio Review — Management Summary

**Prepared:** July 2026 · **Review period:** June 2026 (trailing 12 months) · **Owner:** Enno Heisler

---

## 1. Purpose

We have built a **monthly portfolio management system** for Vanatari: a single,
repeatable process that gives every product a clear verdict each month —
**Scale, Defend, Watch, Incubate, Fix, Harvest, or Exit** — based on explicit,
agreed rules rather than opinion. The verdicts are anchored on contribution
margin (CM1/2/3 %), our AP26 plan targets, sales momentum, and product age.

The goal is the operating rhythm Omar asked for: each month we look at the whole
range through one lens, decide where to invest and where to cut, and keep an
auditable record of why. The system runs on our existing data (Amazon via Nova,
the webshop via Shopify, marketing via Windsor.ai) and produces both an
interactive dashboard and a committed monthly report.

## 2. Headline — June 2026

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Net sales (trailing 12 months) | **€34.7m** |
| Contribution margin CM3 (last 6 months) | **14.9%** vs **21.0%** plan (**−6.1pp**) |
| Products reviewed | **506** SKU × channel (1,738 SKU × marketplace underneath) |
| Products needing action (Fix / Exit) | **191** |

**We are running roughly 6 percentage points below the AP26 margin plan.** On the
last-six-month sales base that gap is on the order of **€1m of contribution**
that the plan expected and we are not yet capturing. The review pinpoints where
it sits and what to do about it.

## 3. What the review found

**a) The webshop is materially less profitable than it appeared.** Until now the
webshop showed a ~44% margin because online marketing cost was not attributed to
it. Having connected **Google Ads and Meta** spend through Windsor.ai, the true
webshop CM3 is **15.6%**, with advertising running at **~28% of sales (TACOS)**.
This is the single biggest correction in the review and it changes the verdict
on ~100 webshop products from "healthy" to "needs work."

**b) A concentrated margin problem on Amazon.** The largest Fix cases are
high-revenue hero products sitting several points below their country targets —
e.g. the **1kg MSM powder** (CM3 8% vs 21% target, ~€0.87m TTM) and **Magnesium
Bisglycinat / VV-VITA-370** (CM3 −5% across several markets, ~€0.84m TTM). A
focused pricing/fee/COGS review of the top ~20 Fix products addresses the bulk
of the plan gap.

**c) An operational alert — wowtamins Amazon stock-out.** The wowtamins Amazon
account has sold **essentially nothing for nine consecutive weeks** (since early
May), with zero stock on hand and **no replenishment inbound**. Estimated lost
sales are **~€250k and growing ~€28k per week**. This is a supply/operations
issue, not a margin issue, and needs resolving before the next review. (Detail
in the accompanying stock-out note.)

**d) A clear action pipeline.** Of 506 product-channels: 62 are healthy and
worth backing (**Scale/Defend**), 89 are on watch, 30 are new and still ramping
(**Incubate**), and **191 need action** (164 Fix, 27 Exit). Each carries a
plain-language reason, so the monthly meeting is a decision meeting, not a
data-gathering one.

## 4. What was built

- **A margin-anchored rules engine.** One transparent rule set (versioned, easy
  to adjust) turns the monthly data into a verdict per product, per channel.
- **Real online profitability.** Google Ads (product-level) and Meta
  (account-level) spend now flow into webshop CM3, so online and Amazon are
  finally compared on the same margin basis.
- **A live dashboard** in Vanatari's brand design: portfolio KPIs, a
  margin-versus-volume "quadrant map" (Winners / Grow / Fix / Low-priority), a
  channel-level decision table with a per-country breakdown, and per-product
  drill-downs.
- **A committed monthly report** that diffs against the prior month, so we can
  see exactly which products changed verdict and why.

## 5. What we need from the business

The review is only as good as its inputs. To close the remaining blind spots:

1. **Real webshop cost inputs** — payment, fulfilment and 3PL fees (currently
   placeholder assumptions). This firms up the webshop margin numbers.
2. **wowtamins webshop connection** — that store is not yet linked, leaving
   ~€0.9m of annual sales unclassified.
3. **Jasnum margin data** — the brand has no margin feed, so it cannot be
   assessed at all today.
4. **Launch dates from the ERP** — ~200 products predate our data history, so
   the "new product" incubation shield can't be applied to them accurately.
5. **A short sign-off on the rule thresholds** (Omar) — the rules are the single
   source of truth; they should reflect management's risk appetite.

## 6. Recommended next steps

- **This month:** resolve the wowtamins Amazon stock-out; launch a margin-repair
  sprint on the top ~20 Fix products (biggest, furthest below target).
- **Adopt the monthly cadence:** refresh data → run the review → decide in one
  meeting → record overrides. The report becomes the standing input to the
  monthly commercial review.
- **Close the data gaps above** so that by the next cycle the webshop margins are
  based on real fees and Jasnum and the wowtamins webshop are in scope.

*The full detail — every product, its verdict and the reasoning — is available in
the interactive dashboard and the committed monthly report.*
